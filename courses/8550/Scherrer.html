The popular Scherrer Equation, which assumes all reflections along a line normal to the reflecting plane. The equation also ignores strain broadening, which is due to variation in lattice parameters.

L =λ K / β cosθ

where:

  • L = mean crystallite dimension (Å)
  • K = constant (typiclly assumed to be 1, but can be changed to slightly lower values to accomodate instrument broadening)
  • β = FWMH of the peak in radians of 2θ (i.e., measure in 2θ and multiply by π/180)

Example: Case of (101) reflection for the quartz structure.

let:

  • FWHM = 0.1°2θ or β = 0.1° 2θ x (π/ 180°)
  • λ = 1.54049 Å
  • Peak position occurs at 26.6° 2θ

then:

L = (1.54059 x 1 ) / [(0.1 x 3.14 / 180) x cos (13.3°)] = 1300Å (130 nm)

With a d-spacing of 3.3446 Å for the (101) this translates to a crystallite size of 388 coherent scattering domains.
